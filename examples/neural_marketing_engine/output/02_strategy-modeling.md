### Psychographics

**Identity:** High-achiever who defines self through performance. Has built an impressive external life but feels disconnected from her body. Identifies as "someone who has it together" publicly but privately feels like she's white-knuckling through each day.

**Pain Architecture:**

| Layer | Pain Point |
|-------|-----------|
| Surface | Chronic tension (jaw, shoulders, chest), poor sleep despite exhaustion, irritability |
| Emotional | Feeling like a fraud — performing calm while internally activated. Shame about "not being able to handle it" |
| Identity | "I've done everything right — therapy, meditation, exercise — why do I still feel this way?" |
| Root Fear | That this is permanent. That high performance requires suffering. That she'll burn out completely before she figures it out. |

**Desire Architecture:**

| Layer | Desire |
|-------|--------|
| Surface | Sleep through the night, release jaw tension, feel calm before meetings |
| Emotional | Feel safe in her own body. Experience joy without guilt. |
| Identity | Become someone who thrives without forcing. A grounded leader, not a reactive one. |
| Root Aspiration | Prove that high performance and deep peace can coexist. |

### Objections & Barriers

| Objection | Response Framework |
|-----------|-------------------|
| "I've tried breathwork apps — they don't work" | Distinguish protocol-based somatic work from generic guided breathing. This is nervous system repatterning, not relaxation. |
| "I don't have time for another program" | 12 minutes/day. The ROI is measured in hours of productivity reclaimed from dysregulation. |
| "$2,497 is expensive" | Frame against cost of burnout (lost income, medical bills, relationship damage). Premium positioning = serious results. |
| "Is this woo-woo?" | Lead with neuroscience: polyvagal theory, HRV data, clinical research on breathwork efficacy. |

## Tone & Voice Guidelines

**Conscious Frequency: Emotional + Authority Hybrid**

| Dimension | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Opening energy | Lead with felt experience. "You know that tightness in your chest before the Monday standup..." |
| Authority signals | Cite polyvagal theory, HRV research, clinical outcomes. Use specific numbers. |
| Language register | Warm, embodied, precise. Not clinical, not guru-like. Think: wise practitioner. |
| Avoid | Toxic positivity, "just breathe" reductionism, gendered language, medical claims |
| CTA energy | Invitational, not urgent. "When you're ready" > "spots are filling up" |
