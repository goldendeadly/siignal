# System Overview

## Purpose

The **Realtor Concierge Engine** provides a unified AI‑driven workflow for managing real‑estate leads from first contact to weekly reporting.  It removes the need for multiple agents by allowing a single AI to handle call intake, lead classification, booking, logging, digest generation, meeting configuration, weekly reporting, quality assurance and optimisation.  The engine emphasises deterministic rules and compliance boundaries to protect the client, minimise missed opportunities and ensure consistent execution.

## Scope of Work

1. **Inbound Call Handling** – Answer inbound calls, ask up to six mandatory questions (intent, area, timeline, pre‑approval/listing status, contact info and booking preference), apply optional questions when appropriate and follow compliance scripts.  Offer a 15‑minute buyer or seller consult when the caller meets the booking criteria.  If booking fails or the caller refuses, follow deterministic fallback rules.
2. **Lead Classification** – Use the captured data to classify leads as HOT, WARM, NURTURE or DISQUALIFY based on deterministic rules (timeline, pre‑approval/listing status, urgency, service area, etc.).  Decide the next action: book consult, callback, nurture follow‑up or disqualify politely.
3. **Logging and Digests** – Record every call outcome in a structured logging sheet with fields like caller name, phone, email, intent, area, timeline bucket, temperature, completeness status, booked status, appointment type and next action.  Generate a daily digest summarising totals, booked appointments, hot leads requiring immediate follow‑up, warm/nurture leads, incomplete records and disqualifications.
4. **Meeting Configuration** – Each week, collect meeting settings (reporting period, niche/service area, meeting types and durations, availability requirements, hot lead handling, qualification/disqualify rules, fit rating criteria, target filters and qualifying questions).  Store these settings for use in scheduling and reporting.
5. **Weekly Reporting** – At the end of each week, produce client‑facing and operator‑facing reports that show leads processed, booked/attended/qualified consults, buyer vs. seller split, priority and at‑risk leads, no‑shows, bottlenecks and suggested focus for next week.  Provide a replacement queue and execution plan if caps/replacement are in play.
6. **Quality Assurance & Tuning** – Run a 12‑scenario test suite to validate classification, booking, escalation and logging behaviours.  Evaluate the results, adjust scripts and rules and determine whether the system is Go/No‑Go for deployment.  Limit tuning changes to protect system stability.
7. **Performance Optimisation** – Analyse logs and reports to identify patterns (lead mix, booking rates, no‑shows, objections, missing info), propose improvements to scripts, triggers, meeting types and fallback rules, and suggest automation opportunities.

## Inputs

| Input | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Run Brief** | Document describing the reporting period, service area, vertical/niche, primary offer and call‑handling preferences. |
| **Business Information** | Phone numbers, appointment types, business hours, after‑hours policies, booking links, hot‑lead alert recipients and tool choices (calendar integration, logging method). |
| **Caller Data** | Details captured during the call: intent, area, timeline bucket, pre‑approval or listing status, contact information, optional questions, and notes. |
| **Weekly Intake Form** | Form capturing meeting settings: meeting types, durations, availability requirements, hot lead handling (transfer or alert), qualification and disqualify rules, fit rating criteria, target filters and qualifying questions. |

## Outputs

| Output | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Logging Sheet** | A CSV or spreadsheet capturing each call’s outcome with all required fields and completeness status. |
| **Booked Appointments** | Scheduled buyer or seller consults, with confirmation and reminders sent via the chosen booking method. |
| **Daily Digest** | An email summarising totals (calls, booked appointments, hot leads, warm/nurture leads, disqualified calls, incomplete records) and listing details for each category. |
| **Weekly Meeting Settings** | A record of the meeting types, availability, rules and targets for the reporting period. |
| **Weekly Reports** | Client‑facing and operator‑facing reports summarising performance and suggesting improvements. |
| **QA Reports** | Results of the QA test suite, including pass/fail status for each scenario and tuning notes. |
| **Recommendations** | Improvements generated by the optimisation context: changes to scripts, rules or processes to increase conversions and reduce missed opportunities. |

## Principles & Rules

- **Determinism** – Classification and fallback rules are deterministic; there is no improvisation.  Conflicting or missing data defaults to WARM classification with the missing fields flagged.
- **Limited Scope** – The system handles call intake, qualification and scheduling.  It does **not** provide legal, financing or commission advice, negotiate deals, discuss contract terms or promise market outcomes.
- **Compliance & Boundaries** – Compliance scripts are mandatory: disclose that you’re an automated concierge, avoid legal/financing predictions, direct emergencies to emergency services and honour opt‑out requests.  Do not exceed six mandatory questions and two appointment types.
- **Logging & Memory** – Every call outcome must be logged.  Daily digests and weekly reports are produced from these logs.  Incomplete records require follow‑up to capture missing data.
- **Consistency** – Use the same script, classification rules and templates across runs.  Tuning changes are limited to protect system stability.
- **Separation of Contexts** – Keep system definition separate from workspace files.  Contexts should operate independently, reading only the necessary inputs and producing defined outputs.
