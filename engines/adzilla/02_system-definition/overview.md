# System Overview

This document provides a deeper explanation of the AdZilla Advertising Engine.  It elaborates on the purpose, inputs, outputs, contexts and principles that guide the system.

## Purpose

AdZilla exists to streamline the process of creating, deploying and optimising advertising campaigns.  It takes a structured approach to turning an offer into a set of targeted, compliant, platform‑specific ads, complete with tracking and optimisation recommendations.  By following a repeatable workflow, marketers and creators can focus on strategy and creative thinking while the system handles organisation and documentation.

## Inputs

The system requires the following inputs to begin a run:

1. **Offer Details:** Core description of the product/service, including features, benefits and unique selling proposition.
2. **Audience Persona:** Demographic and psychographic information about the target audience, pain points, desires and objections.
3. **Resonance Mode / Brand Voice:** The tone of the campaign (Authority, Emotional, Technical, Viral, Educational, Hybrid) that dictates how messages are phrased.
4. **Marketing Objective:** The goal of the campaign (Awareness, Lead Generation, Sales, Brand Loyalty, Retargeting, etc.).
5. **Platform Selection:** Which advertising channels to use (Facebook, Instagram, Google Ads, LinkedIn, YouTube, TikTok, etc.).
6. **Budget & Timing:** Total budget, daily/lifetime spend and campaign timeframe.

## Outputs

AdZilla produces a wide variety of outputs, including:

1. **Value Proposition Assets** – concise statements, bullet points, hooks and taglines that summarise why the offer matters to the audience.
2. **Ad Copy Files** – channel‑specific ads with headline, body copy, call to action and creative suggestions for each selected platform.
3. **Targeting Plans** – segmentation parameters, budget allocations and schedule recommendations tailored to each platform.
4. **Tracking Tables** – master campaign trackers and individual ad performance trackers capturing metrics such as impressions, clicks, CTR, conversions and ROI.
5. **Optimisation & Expansion Proposals** – suggestions for A/B tests, retargeting campaigns, cross‑selling, upselling, lookalike audiences and automation improvements.

## Context Areas

The workflow is divided into seven context areas, each representing a distinct phase of work:

1. **offer-intake** – Collect run details and document offer information, objectives and constraints.
2. **market-analysis** – Analyse the audience, competitors and brand voice; identify benefits and differentiators.
3. **creative-generation** – Produce the core messaging assets: value propositions, benefit bullets, hooks and taglines.
4. **ad-assembly** – Compose platform‑specific ads based on the core assets, adapting messaging to each channel’s conventions.
5. **targeting-plan** – Define audience segments, budgets, geographic targeting and scheduling for each platform.
6. **performance-tracking** – Log and review performance metrics, maintain trackers, verify canonical lines and document reflections.
7. **optimisation-expansion** – Recommend tests, retargeting and automation to enhance future runs and leverage successful elements.

## Principles

AdZilla follows these guiding principles:

- **Context‑Driven Work:** Each context has a clear purpose and defined outputs.  By separating tasks into contexts, the system ensures focus and reduces cognitive load.
- **Derived Structure:** Context names and files are derived from the advertising workflow, not imposed arbitrarily.
- **Preserve Source Integrity:** Always record original offer details and analysis; never overwrite source information.
- **Consistent Naming:** Use the naming conventions provided to make automation and navigation easy.
- **Data‑Driven Optimisation:** Track and reflect on performance to inform future campaigns.
- **Scalability:** The system supports single campaigns as well as large, multi‑offer advertising programmes by repeating and archiving runs.
