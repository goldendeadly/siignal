# Platform Explosion Workflow

This document details the procedure for generating multi‑platform outputs in the **Platform Explosion** phase.  The objective is to produce a comprehensive set of platform‑specific assets while maintaining consistency with the original source, resonance mode, SEO signals and CTA structure.

## Steps

1. **Collect the core assets**
   - Copy `summary.md`, `meta-description.md` and `key-highlights.md` from the core-generation workspace.
   - Keep the primary keyword, LSI terms and canonical line close at hand.

2. **Determine active platforms**
   - Based on the run brief and project requirements, decide which platforms to generate content for.  Typical platforms include:
     - Twitter/X (threads, tweets)
     - LinkedIn (articles, posts, carousels)
     - Instagram (carousels, reels)
     - YouTube (long‑form scripts and micro‑clips)
     - Quora (answers)
     - Facebook (captions)
     - Pinterest (pin titles and descriptions)
     - Medium/Substack/Blogger/Dev.to/Hashnode (articles)

3. **Generate platform outlines**
   - For each selected platform, outline the content structure.  Use the following patterns as a guide:
     - **Twitter/X:** 1 hook tweet, a series of insight tweets (4–6), a metaphorical reflection, and a final CTA tweet.  Hashtag set (3–5 relevant tags).
     - **LinkedIn:** 1 article (200–300 words), 1 quote‑based post, 1 story post, each ending with an engagement CTA (“What’s your perspective?”).
     - **Instagram:** 7 carousel scripts (one per key highlight) with title slide, 3–4 summary slides and a final CTA slide.  Captions ≤150 words with an engagement CTA (“Which resonates most with you?”).
     - **YouTube:** Outline a long‑form video (7–10 minutes) with 7 sections (based on key highlights) and clear hooks and transitions.  List micro‑clip topics.
     - **Quora:** Draft a 300–400 word answer addressing a common question related to the post; include examples and frameworks from the source.
     - **Facebook:** Write one or two short captions (≤150 words) focusing on relatable takeaways and emotional storytelling.
     - **Pinterest:** Create a pin title (≤60 characters) and description (≤200 characters) with actionable keywords and canonical link.
     - **Medium/Substack/Blogger/Dev.to/Hashnode:** Slightly shorten the intro, maintain narrative flow, and close with a call to action.  Always include the canonical line.

4. **Write platform‑specific drafts**
   - Use the outlines to write the actual content for each platform.
   - Incorporate the resonance mode (authority, emotional, technical, viral, educational or hybrid) selected in the run brief.
   - Layer keywords, hashtags and CTAs as appropriate.  Vary CTA tiering (soft, engagement, conversion) within and across platforms.
   - Embed the canonical line at the end or where appropriate.

5. **Save the drafts**
   - For each platform, save your drafts into the run workspace under `platform-explosion/` using the naming convention `[topic]-[platform]-[asset-type]-v1.md`.  For example:
     - `adaptive-prompting-twitter-thread-v1.md`
     - `adaptive-prompting-linkedin-article-v1.md`
     - `adaptive-prompting-youtube-script-v1.md`

6. **Review and adjust**
   - Check that the tone aligns with the resonance mode and target audience.
   - Verify that hashtags and keywords are relevant and not overused.
   - Ensure the canonical line is present and the CTA drives readers back to the main blog or next action.

7. **Hand off to Tracking & Canon**
   - Once drafts are complete, proceed to the Tracking & Canon phase to log outputs, set publish dates and monitor performance.

## Outputs

- A suite of platform‑specific drafts saved in the run workspace, each containing hooks, insights, transitions, hashtags, CTAs and canonical statements.
