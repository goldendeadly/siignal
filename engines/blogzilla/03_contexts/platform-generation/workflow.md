# Platform Generation Workflow

1. **Gather inputs** – collect the summary, meta description, highlights, tone profile and keyword map for the run.
2. **Select platforms** – decide which platforms to generate for based on the run brief (e.g. X/Twitter, LinkedIn, Instagram, YouTube, Quora, Facebook, Pinterest, Medium, Substack, Blogger, Dev.to).
3. **Draft outputs per platform** – use the platform guidelines to create drafts:
   - **X/Twitter** – 7–10 tweets (≤280 characters) summarising insights; include 3–5 relevant hashtags and one CTA.
   - **LinkedIn** – one article (200–300 words) and two posts (framework and storytelling styles), each ending with an engagement CTA.
   - **Instagram** – carousel concepts (title slide, 3–4 summary slides, final CTA); captions ≤150 words with engagement prompts.
   - **YouTube** – outline a 7–10 minute long‑form video and 7 micro‑clip segments; include hooks, transitions and CTA.
   - **Quora** – 300–400 word answer to a common question; include two examples or frameworks and a CTA.
   - **Facebook** – 1–2 captions ≤150 words emphasising relatability; include suggested visual and comment prompt.
   - **Pinterest** – one pin title ≤60 characters and description ≤200 characters; suggest a visual.
   - **Medium/Substack/Blogger/Dev.to** – adapt the blog into shorter or adjusted formats with canonical links and narrative flow.
4. **Apply resonance mode and CTA tier** – ensure the tone matches the selected resonance mode and the CTA tier (Soft, Engagement, Conversion) is appropriate.
5. **Include canonical link & hashtags** – append the canonical/backlink line and relevant hashtag sets.
6. **Save drafts** – save each platform’s outputs into their respective files within the run folder (e.g. `thread-x-[topic]-draft-v1.md`, `carousel-instagram-[topic]-draft-v1.md`, `video-youtube-[topic]-outline-v1.md`).
7. **Hand off** – once drafts are saved, move the run to `amplification-expansion` or directly to `tracking-memory` if no expansion is needed.
