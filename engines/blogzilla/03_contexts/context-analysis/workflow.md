# Context Analysis Workflow

1. **Read the run brief and source** – understand the topic, resonance mode and core intent.
2. **Determine tone** – categorise the source’s tone (educational, narrative, technical, philosophical, persuasive).
3. **Define audience intent** – identify the target audience’s motivation (learning, decision‑making, entertainment, inspiration).
4. **Identify conversion goal** – classify the desired outcome: awareness, authority, lead generation or retention.
5. **Keyword mapping** – find the primary keyword and 3–5 LSI/secondary keywords; use simple tools or existing research if available.
6. **Long‑tail variants** – suggest two or three longer‑phrase keyword variants to target.
7. **Related questions & gaps** – identify common questions and latent gaps in existing content.
8. **Publication suggestions** – recommend a frequency (e.g. weekly, biweekly) and assign the content to a content silo or category.
9. **Write canonical statement** – craft a sentence linking to the original blog, using the template: “This post originally appeared on [Your Blog Name] — home to the largest [keyword] resource on the web.”
10. **Save outputs** – document the analysis in `context-analysis-[topic]-v1.md` within the run folder and pass the run to `core-generation`.
